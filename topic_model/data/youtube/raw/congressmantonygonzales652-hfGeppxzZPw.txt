THIS LEGISLATION COMES AT A CRITICAL TIME AS WE CELEBRATE THE ACCOMPLISHMENTS OF THE LATINO COMMUNITY DURING HISPANIC HERITAGE MONTH. FROM 1909 TO 1965, THE BLACKWELL SCHOOL IN MARFA, TEXAS, WAS THE ONLY PUBLIC SCHOOL FOR THE CITY'S MEXICAN AMERICAN CHILDREN. ALTHOUGH THERE WAS NO STATE LAW THAT MANDATED SEPARATED SCHOOLS FOR HISPANIC STUDENTS, IT WAS COMMON PRACTICE FOR THEM TO BE TAUGHT AT DIFFERENT FACILITIES. THIS MEANT THAT THE BLACKWELL SCHOOL WAS THE ONLY PLACE WHERE CHILDREN OF MEXICAN DESCENT COULD BE EDUCATED AND HAVE A SHOT AT THE AMERICAN DREAM. TODAY THE BLACKWELL SCHOOL REMINDS US OF THE RESILIENCY THE MEXICAN AMERICAN COMMUNITY HAS DISPLAYED THROUGHOUT OUR HISTORY.
