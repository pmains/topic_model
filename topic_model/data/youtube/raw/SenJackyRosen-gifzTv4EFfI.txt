VEGETATION-- AFTER THE BREAK. LOOKING TO OUR COMMUNITY -- NEVADA IS SET TO RECEIVE NEARLY 400-THOUSAND DOLLARS TO IMPROVE WATER ACCESS. THE FUNDS ARE COMING FROM THE BIPARTISAN INFRASTRUCTURE LAW. HERE IN NORTHERN NEVADA -- THE TRUCKEE-CARSON IRRIGATION DISTRICT IN FALLON IS SET TO GET 100-THOUSAND. THE REST OF THE MONEY IS HEADED TO CLARK COUNTY. ACCORDING TO NEVADA SENATOR JACKY ROSEN -- THE FUNDS ARE SET TO HELP COMMUNITIES MONITOR WATER LEVELS
