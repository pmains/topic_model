R STATES RECEIVED AN "F" GRADE IN  A REPORT FOCUSED ON DRINKING WATER AT SCHOOLS, GEORGIA SENATOR JON OSSOFF PUSHED FOR MORE FUNDING TO REPLACE LEAD PIPES AT PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN GEORGIA AND NATIONWIDE. HIS EFFORTS LED  TO THE SENATE PASSING THE  "DRINKING  WATER AND WASTEWATER INFRASTRUCTURE ACT" BACK IN  APRIL. THE BILL INCREASES  FUNDING FOR EPA'S PROGRAM TO 100 MILLION ANNUALLY-- AND PROVIDE SCHOOLS WITH 200 MILLION IN THE NEXT FIVE YEARS.
